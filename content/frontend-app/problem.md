Frozen shoulder affects approximately **3–5% of adults globally**, but in Hong Kong 
the picture is more pressing: **90% of working adults report neck and shoulder pain**, 
yet fewer than 20% seek professional help. With 33.3% of Hong Kong's population 
projected to be aged 65 or above by 2039, the demand for accessible, at-home 
rehabilitation tools will only grow.

The clinical gap is well-documented. Only **35% of physiotherapy patients fully follow 
their prescribed home exercise plans** — without a daily guide or reminder, routines 
are quickly abandoned. Older patients forget up to **37% of a longer exercise list** 
after a single teaching session, even with written instruction sheets as a memory aid.

> The problem is not a lack of medical knowledge — it is the absence of a tool that 
> bridges the clinic and everyday life, using hardware people already own.

Existing solutions either require expensive wearables, rely on passive video 
demonstrations with no real-time feedback, or demand technical literacy that older 
adults may not have. This app proposes a new direction for Hong Kong's physiotherapy 
and rehabilitation sector: a motion-guided coach that runs entirely on the phone 
already in your pocket — no equipment, no subscriptions, no barriers to entry.
