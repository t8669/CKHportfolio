About Me

Bio

Final-year Creative Media student at City University of Hong Kong, building at the intersection of software, hardware, and interaction design.

Most developers write code. Most designers make interfaces. I do both — and when the problem calls for it, I fabricate the physical object too. My FYP combined custom Arduino hardware, Godot-powered projections, and 9 original IP characters into a single installation. My other work spans Firebase-backed Android apps and sensor-driven rehabilitation tools.

I work with AI as a force multiplier — not to replace thinking, but to compress the distance between a problem and a working solution. That means directing AI with real-world context it cannot source itself: recording actual motion data to anchor algorithm design, feeding domain knowledge that has no training set. The goal is always the same — spend less attention on implementation mechanics, and more on the problem that actually matters.
